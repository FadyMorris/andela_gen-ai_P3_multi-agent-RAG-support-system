# Company Financial Policies and Procedures Manual

## 1. Introduction to Finance Department

The Finance Department is responsible for managing all financial operations, including budgeting, accounting, reporting, payroll, accounts payable and receivable, and financial planning. Our mission is to provide accurate, timely financial information to support strategic decision-making across the organization.

## 2. General Financial Principles

All financial transactions must adhere to Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (GAAP), relevant tax regulations, and company policies. Financial integrity, accuracy, and transparency are non-negotiable. Every employee handling company funds must maintain detailed records and follow proper authorization procedures.

## 3. Accounting Standards and Compliance

We comply with GAAP, SOX (Sarbanes-Oxley), and applicable federal and state tax regulations. Annual independent audits are conducted by external auditors. All financial statements must be reviewed and approved by the CFO before release. Compliance violations are escalated immediately to the Audit Committee.

## 4. Chart of Accounts Structure

The Chart of Accounts is organized hierarchically: Assets (100-199), Liabilities (200-299), Equity (300-399), Revenue (400-499), Cost of Goods Sold (500-599), Operating Expenses (600-699), and Other Income/Expenses (700-799). All transactions must be coded to appropriate accounts. Changes to the Chart of Accounts require Finance Director approval.

## 5. Budgeting Process and Timeline

Annual budgeting begins in August for the following fiscal year. Department heads submit detailed budget requests by September 15th. The Finance Department consolidates requests and presents the draft budget to the CFO by October 1st. The board approves the final budget by November 30th. Monthly budget-to-actual reviews occur on the 5th business day of each month.

## 6. Budget Approval Authority

Individual employees may approve purchases up to $500. Department managers may approve up to $5,000. Directors may approve up to $25,000. The CFO approves purchases between $25,000 and $100,000. The CEO and Board approve purchases exceeding $100,000. All approval levels must verify available budget before authorization.

## 7. Expense Reimbursement Policy

Employees must submit expense reports within 30 days of incurring expenses. All expenses require itemized receipts. Personal card transactions are preferred; cash reimbursement is discouraged. Meals are reimbursed up to $15 breakfast, $20 lunch, $30 dinner (with client). Hotel stays are limited to $200/night in most locations, $300 in major cities. All reimbursements are processed within 10 business days of approval.

## 8. Travel and Entertainment Expenses

Domestic flights must use economy class. International flights over 6 hours may use premium economy. Hotel reservations should be made through the corporate travel booking system. Ground transportation includes taxi, rideshare, or rental cars. Meal expenses require itemization and business purpose. Entertainment expenses must include attendees' names and business purpose. All travel must be approved in advance by the department manager.

## 9. Procurement and Purchasing Procedures

All purchases must use the approved vendor list when available. Competitive bids are required for purchases exceeding $10,000. Purchase orders must be created for all non-recurring purchases over $1,000. Three-way matching (PO, receipt, invoice) is required before payment. Emergency purchases require CFO approval and must be documented within 24 hours.

## 10. Vendor Management and Selection

New vendors must be approved by the Procurement team. Vendors must provide W-9 forms and meet insurance requirements. Price negotiations are conducted by Procurement. Vendor performance is reviewed quarterly. Non-performing vendors are placed on probation or removed from the approved list. All vendor contracts must be reviewed by Legal before execution.

## 11. Accounts Payable Procedures

Invoices are matched to POs and receipts before payment processing. Payment terms are Net 30 by default; early payment discounts are evaluated based on cash position. Invoices are paid on a weekly schedule: Mondays for Friday deadline submissions. Disputed invoices are escalated to the vendor and Finance Director. Monthly reconciliation of AP subsidiary ledger occurs by the 5th business day.

## 12. Accounts Receivable Management

Invoices are issued within 24 hours of order fulfillment or service delivery. Credit terms are Net 30 standard; Net 60 for preferred customers. Collection notices are sent at 15, 30, and 60 days past due. Accounts over 90 days past due are escalated to the Finance Director. Bad debt reserves are evaluated quarterly. Credit limits are set based on customer creditworthiness and payment history.

## 13. Credit Policy and Customer Creditworthiness

Credit applications require business license, references, and financial statements. Personal guarantees may be required for smaller companies. Credit limits are established based on annual sales projections. Customers exceeding credit limits require cash payment or secured credit. Credit line increases require Finance Director approval. Credit review is conducted annually.

## 14. Payroll Administration

Payroll is processed biweekly on Fridays. Timesheets must be submitted by Wednesday 5 PM. Managers approve timesheets by Thursday noon. Payroll cutoff is Thursday 3 PM. All salary information is confidential. Base salaries are reviewed annually in November. Merit raises are allocated from the merit budget. Cost-of-living adjustments occur as determined by the Board.

## 15. Payroll Tax Compliance

Federal income tax, Social Security, and Medicare are withheld per IRS tables. State and local taxes are withheld as required. Payroll tax returns are filed timely (941 quarterly, W-2 annually). IRS employment tax deposits are made semi-weekly. Tax credits are tracked and utilized. Year-end reconciliation ensures compliance. Any tax notices are immediately reported to the CFO.

## 16. Employee Benefits Administration

Health insurance is provided with company contribution of 80% for individual, 70% for family. Dental and vision coverage is 100% employer-paid. Life insurance is 2x annual salary, paid by company. 401(k) matching is 100% up to 3% of salary. FSA and HSA accounts are available. Benefits are deducted pre-tax where applicable. Open enrollment occurs in November for January effective date.

## 17. Retirement Planning and 401(k) Management

All employees are eligible for 401(k) after 90 days. Company matches 100% of contributions up to 3% of salary. Vesting is 25% per year, fully vested after 4 years. Plan is administered by Fidelity. Annual compliance testing ensures non-discrimination. Statement distribution occurs quarterly. Participants may take loans per plan terms. Rollovers are permitted per IRS regulations.

## 18. Debt Management and Credit Facilities

The company maintains a $5 million revolving line of credit with First National Bank at prime + 1.5%. Current outstanding balance is $1.2 million. Interest-only payments are made monthly; principal is due upon maturity. The CFO approves all borrowings. Debt covenants include maintaining a minimum current ratio of 1.5:1. Annual debt review occurs with the Board in Q4.

## 19. Cash Management and Banking

Concentration bank account is maintained at First National Bank. All subsidiaries deposit to a single account. Cash position is reviewed daily. Investment of excess cash is authorized per the Investment Policy. Idle cash exceeding $500,000 is invested short-term. Bank reconciliation occurs monthly within 5 business days. All bank accounts require dual authorization for wire transfers.

## 20. Investment Policy and Authorization

Excess cash may be invested in money market funds, CDs, and short-term bonds. Target allocation is 80% money market, 20% bonds. Minimum credit rating is A-. Investment decisions are made by the CFO and Treasury team. Annual performance review against benchmarks. Authorized institutions include major banks and investment firms. Concentration limits prevent over-reliance on single institutions.

## 21. Fixed Asset Management and Depreciation

Fixed assets are capitalized if exceeding $5,000 and having useful life over one year. Depreciation methods: straight-line for buildings (39 years), MACRS for equipment. Assets are tracked in the fixed asset register with location and custodian. Annual physical inventory is conducted in December. Disposals are recorded and authorized by the Finance Director. Depreciation expense is reviewed for accuracy quarterly.

## 22. Inventory Valuation and Management

Inventory is valued at lower of cost or market (LOCOM). FIFO method is used for valuation. Physical inventory count occurs annually in December. Shrinkage variance exceeding 2% is investigated. Obsolete inventory is written off with department manager and Finance Director approval. Inventory insurance is reviewed annually. Monthly aging and turnover analysis is conducted.

## 23. Capitalization vs. Expense Criteria

Expenditures over $5,000 are capitalized if useful life exceeds one year. Repairs and maintenance are expensed. Improvements extending useful life are capitalized. Betterments improving functionality are capitalized. Software licenses over $10,000 are capitalized and amortized over 3 years. Subscriptions are expensed. All capitalization decisions are reviewed by the Accounting Manager.

## 24. Revenue Recognition Policy

Revenue is recognized when performance obligations are satisfied. For product sales, revenue is recognized upon delivery. For services, revenue is recognized as services are delivered. Subscription revenue is recognized ratably over the subscription period. Advance payments are recorded as deferred revenue. Revenue is net of returns and allowances. Quarterly revenue analysis includes trend analysis and variance investigation.

## 25. Expense Recognition and Accrual Accounting

Expenses are recognized in the period incurred, regardless of payment timing. Accruals are recorded for known obligations. Estimates are made for utilities, bonuses, and warranties. Quarterly accrual analysis ensures no material unrecorded obligations. Month-end cutoff procedures ensure proper period allocation. Accrual reversals occur when actual amounts are known.

## 26. Internal Controls and Segregation of Duties

Purchasing, receiving, and payment processing are segregated. Authorization is separate from execution. Monthly reconciliations identify discrepancies. Random transaction audits are conducted. Restricted access to financial systems is enforced. Password controls and multi-factor authentication are required. Annual internal control assessment is conducted by the Finance Director.

## 27. Financial Reporting and Statements

Monthly financial statements are issued by the 5th business day. Statements include Balance Sheet, Income Statement, and Cash Flow Statement. Notes to financial statements explain significant items. Quarterly management discussion analyzes variances. Year-end audited statements are issued within 45 days. Comparisons to prior periods highlight trends. Statements are reviewed by the Audit Committee.

## 28. Balance Sheet Analysis and Interpretation

The Balance Sheet shows financial position at a specific date. Current assets should exceed current liabilities by at least 1.5x. Working capital is monitored for operational sufficiency. Fixed asset turnover is tracked quarterly. Debt-to-equity ratio is maintained below 1.5x per covenant. Changes in significant accounts are documented and explained. The CFO reviews balance sheet health monthly.

## 29. Income Statement Analysis and Performance Metrics

The Income Statement shows results for the period. Gross margin is monitored as percentage of revenue. Operating expenses are analyzed for efficiency and control. EBITDA margin is tracked as key performance metric. The P&L is compared monthly to budget and prior year. Department-level P&Ls are shared with managers. Top-line and bottom-line growth are tracked quarterly.

## 30. Cash Flow Statement and Liquidity Analysis

The Cash Flow Statement shows changes in cash position. Operating cash flow should exceed net income (quality of earnings). Investing activities are monitored for capital deployment. Financing activities track debt and equity changes. Working capital changes are analyzed for efficiency. Free cash flow is calculated and tracked quarterly. Cash conversion cycle is monitored.

## 31. Key Financial Ratios and Performance Indicators

Current Ratio measures short-term liquidity. Quick Ratio excludes inventory for conservative measure. Debt-to-Equity Ratio shows leverage. Return on Assets measures efficiency. Return on Equity measures shareholder returns. Gross Margin indicates pricing power. Operating Margin shows operational efficiency. Asset Turnover measures asset utilization. Quarterly variance analysis reviews all ratios.

## 32. Variance Analysis and Budget Monitoring

Monthly budget-to-actual variance is analyzed for amounts exceeding $10,000 or 15%. Explanations are provided for significant variances. Trends are identified and tracked. Full-year forecasts are adjusted based on year-to-date performance. Budget updates are approved by the CFO if variances exceed 5% for the year. Department managers receive variance reports monthly.

## 33. Forecasting and Financial Planning

Rolling 13-week cash flow forecast is maintained. Annual revenue forecast includes seasonal adjustments. Expense forecast accounts for known changes. Scenario planning is conducted for best/worst case outcomes. Headcount projections drive salary expense forecasts. Capital expenditure plans are included in financial forecasts. Forecast updates occur monthly and are approved by the CFO.

## 34. Tax Planning and Compliance

Quarterly estimated tax payments are made to federal and state authorities. Tax provision is calculated and recorded in financial statements. Tax credits (R&D, work opportunity) are tracked and utilized. Entity structure is reviewed annually for tax efficiency. Year-end tax planning occurs in October. Tax returns are filed timely. Any IRS or state tax notices are immediately escalated.

## 35. Sales Tax Compliance and Remittance

Sales tax is collected per state regulations. Tax is remitted monthly to applicable states. Resale certificates are maintained for tax-exempt purchases. Audit trail is maintained for sales tax calculations. Quarterly reconciliation ensures no discrepancies. Tax rate changes are implemented immediately. Nexus analysis is conducted annually for new markets.

## 36. Cost Accounting and Product Costing

Direct materials are tracked by product. Direct labor is allocated based on time tracking. Manufacturing overhead is allocated based on labor hours. Standard costs are established and reviewed quarterly. Actual costs are compared to standards; variances are investigated. Joint cost allocation methodology is documented. Product profitability is analyzed monthly.

## 37. General Ledger Management and Reconciliation

General Ledger is the official record of all transactions. Account reconciliations occur monthly by the 5th business day. Bank reconciliation is completed within 3 business days of month-end. AP and AR aging reports are reviewed for accuracy. Fixed asset register is reconciled to GL monthly. Intercompany accounts are settled monthly. Any unreconciled items are escalated immediately.

## 38. Month-End Close Procedures

Close procedures begin on the last business day of the month. All transactions must be recorded before cutoff. Journal entries are reviewed and approved by the Accounting Manager. Reconciliations are completed and documented. Accruals are recorded and verified. Financial statements are prepared and reviewed. Audit trail is maintained for all close activities. Close typically completes by the 5th business day.

## 39. Year-End Close and Audit Preparation

Year-end close begins December 15th. All transactions must be recorded before December 31st. Complete financial statements are prepared. Account balances are reconciled in detail. Permanent journal entries are prepared and documented. Audit readiness items are completed: documentation, working papers, and schedules. Auditors begin fieldwork early January. All audit findings are resolved timely.

## 40. External Audit Process and Management

Annual external audit is conducted by [Auditor Firm]. Audit planning occurs in December. Fieldwork typically runs 2-3 weeks in January. Management representations are provided at audit conclusion. Audit findings and recommendations are discussed with the Audit Committee. Any material weaknesses are addressed immediately. Audit fee is budgeted annually.

## 41. Internal Audit and Compliance Monitoring

Internal audit program includes financial controls, operational efficiency, and regulatory compliance. Testing includes transaction sampling, controls evaluation, and compliance verification. Audit reports are issued quarterly with findings and recommendations. Management develops corrective action plans. Follow-up audits verify remediation. Annual audit plan is approved by the Audit Committee.

## 42. Financial Data Security and Access Controls

Financial system access is restricted to authorized personnel. Multi-factor authentication is required. Role-based access ensures proper segregation of duties. Sensitive data is encrypted at rest and in transit. User access reviews occur quarterly. Terminated employees are removed from systems immediately. Audit logs are reviewed monthly for suspicious activity. Data backups occur daily with offsite storage.

## 43. Fraud Prevention and Detection

Code of conduct requires ethical behavior and fraud prevention. Conflict of interest disclosures are required annually. Anonymous hotline allows reporting of concerns. Surprise audits are conducted periodically. High-risk transactions are reviewed. Vendor and employee background checks are conducted. Insurance includes fidelity coverage for key personnel. Any fraud is reported to law enforcement.

## 44. Accounts Receivable Aging and Collection

AR aging report is generated monthly. Invoices outstanding 15+ days are reviewed. Collection calls are made for past-due accounts. Dunning process includes letters at 30 and 60 days. Write-offs require Finance Director approval. Reserve for doubtful accounts is adjusted quarterly. Customer payment patterns are analyzed. Collection efficiency is tracked as days sales outstanding.

## 45. Accounts Payable Aging and Payment Planning

AP aging report is generated weekly. Invoices are prioritized by payment date. Early payment discounts are evaluated (terms like 2/10 N30). Payment schedule optimizes cash position. Disputed invoices are researched and resolved. Vendor relationship management maintains payment reliability. AP turnover is benchmarked against industry. Monthly AP reconciliation ensures accuracy.

## 46. Expense Management Systems and Approval Workflows

Expense reports are submitted through the online system. Mobile app allows photo capture of receipts. Automatic routing ensures proper approvals. System validates coding and policy compliance. Reimbursement is processed automatically after approval. Recurring expenses are flagged for review. Department-level spend analysis identifies trends. Policy violations are flagged for manager review.

## 47. Corporate Credit Card Programs

Corporate cards are issued to authorized employees. $5,000 monthly spending limit per card. Reconciliation occurs monthly against submissions. Non-compliant expenses are charged back to cardholder. Card misuse results in immediate cancellation and discipline. Monthly statements are reviewed for fraud. Card fees are negotiated based on volume. Cardholder training is mandatory.

## 48. Insurance and Risk Management

General liability insurance provides $2 million coverage. Property insurance covers all owned assets. Workers' compensation insurance is required. Directors and officers liability provides $10 million coverage. Cyber liability insurance covers data breaches. Annual insurance review ensures adequate coverage. Claims are reported immediately to insurance carrier. Risk assessment identifies mitigation opportunities.

## 49. Compliance and Regulatory Requirements

GDPR compliance for EU customer data. CCPA compliance for California customer data. SOX compliance for internal controls. FCPA compliance for international transactions. Data retention policy ensures timely disposal. Privacy policy is reviewed annually. Compliance training is required for all employees. Annual compliance audit is conducted. Any violations are reported to Legal immediately.

## 50. Finance Department Roles and Responsibilities

**Controller:** Oversees accounting, financial reporting, and internal controls.

**Finance Manager:** Manages accounts payable, accounts receivable, and general ledger.

**Financial Analyst:** Prepares forecasts, analyzes variance, and supports planning.

**Payroll Specialist:** Administers payroll, tax withholding, and benefits.

**Tax Coordinator:** Manages tax planning, compliance, and reporting.

**Audit Coordinator:** Supports internal and external audits, compliance monitoring.

## 51. Financial System Architecture and Technology

ERP system is NetSuite, configured for multi-entity operations. GL module handles all accounting transactions. AP and AR modules streamline payment and collection processes. Expense management integration reduces manual entry. Payroll integration ensures accurate labor cost allocation. Reporting dashboard provides real-time visibility. System access logs are reviewed monthly for integrity.

## 52. Quarterly Business Review and Reporting

Quarterly review occurs 5 days after month-end close. CFO presents financial results to executive team. Revenue trends and profitability are discussed. Cash position and liquidity are analyzed. Budget variance explanations are provided. Forward guidance is updated. Board package is prepared for director review. Action items are assigned with accountability.

## 53. Financial Planning and Strategic Analysis

Three-year financial forecast drives strategic planning. Scenario analysis evaluates alternative strategies. Investment opportunities are evaluated using NPV and IRR. Acquisition targets are assessed for financial fit. Pricing strategy is informed by cost and market analysis. Market expansion decisions are supported by financial analysis. Annual strategic plan is approved by the Board.

## 54. Capital Expenditure Planning and Approval

Capital requests are submitted in annual budget process. Projects exceeding $50,000 require detailed ROI analysis. Payback period and IRR are calculated. Strategic alignment is evaluated. Depreciation impact on future earnings is modeled. Project tracking includes milestones and spending control. Post-implementation review validates financial assumptions.

## 55. Employee Education and Finance Training

Monthly finance training sessions cover topics like expense reporting and approval workflows. New employee onboarding includes financial policy overview. Finance team conducts quarterly business partner meetings with department managers. Annual compliance training covers fraud prevention and data security. Advanced training in financial analysis is available to interested employees.
